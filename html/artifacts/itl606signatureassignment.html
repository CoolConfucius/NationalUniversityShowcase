1. You have been asked to read and learn about Universal Design for Learning. The three basic principles focus on multiple means of (a) representation, (b) expression, and (c) engagement. Discuss with your partners.

Write a brief persuasive paragraph advocating for the adoption and use of the principles of Universal Design for Learning. (Maximum 150 words)

 

Universal Designs for learning gives all students access to the curriculum by reducing the barriers. UDL meets state standards, allows flexibility, and is set up to support the needs, strengths, interests, and preferred ways of learning for a range of diverse learners (Ralabate, 2018). It gives all students equal opportunity to succeed, including learners with disabilities (Morin, 2018). This is ideal for Mr. Jones’s class of diverse learners where he can use various methods of presenting lessons, allow a variety of ways for students to express what they know, and keep them engaged using multiple means of motivation.

 


Write a brief persuasive paragraph advocating against the adoption and use of the principles of Universal Design for Learning. (Maximum 150 words)

Universal Design for Learning is geared toward helping all students in the classroom succeeding by specific adaptations.  It gets harder to adapt UDL in the classroom as class sizes increase, and more variety of needs are presented.  UDL would not only create stress to the teacher in providing for all, but could possibly create suffering and hardship in the students’ success. A well thought out teaching style may be effective for eighty percent of the students in a relatively large class. An attempt to make learning optimal for the other twenty percent will likely run the risk of the other students losing motivation, patience, or clarity on the class material. Instead of developing a “one-size-fits-all” teaching style, it may be more effective to teach in a way that is effective for the majority while spending time supporting individuals who need additional attention.
