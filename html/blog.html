Bio

 

I'm Ben, born in 1990. My dream is to become a published fiction writer. I was born in San Mateo, but my family and I moved to Taiwan when I was just a toddler. I went to schools in Taiwan and graduated from an international school in 2010. After that, I went to California and started attending Santa Clara University for my undergrad.

 

I started as an English major, switched to a Psychology major with a minor in Creative Writing, then switched to a Liberal Studies major, then switched to a Computer Science major. In 2015, I graduated from SCU with a Bachelor’s in Computer Science and a minor in Creative Writing.

 

After graduating college, I’ve attended software developer training programs. After that, I’ve been doing software development. I found myself very unhappy with software development, and my intuition had been strongly urging me to switch paths. Reflecting, I’ve decided that I would be happier as a teacher than as a software developer.

 

Why I chose teaching as a career

 

When I was a student, I had very negative experiences in Middle and High school. Being on the autism spectrum, I was an easy target for both students and teachers. I want to be a teacher, so I can help students feel safe and have positive experiences in school.

 

Not wanting my computer science / programming training to go to waste, I want to teach computer science and programming to K12 schools. With the increase in demand for computer literacy, there should be a demand in computer science education. My current understanding is, not all schools teach computer science. And in those that do, computer science doesn’t nearly have the same weight as other subjects. To my knowledge, there isn’t a CSET in computer science, so I took my CSET in Mathematics.

 

My personality type, teaching and learning style

 

I have gotten various results from multiple tests, but the personality type I identify with the most is INTP. I am highly introverted, and this is consistent across tests. I have an appreciation for both big pictures and details. I value being logical, practical, and honest.

I have learned best by working with clear, unambiguous, instructions. I find examining examples and demonstrations and identifying patterns helps me learn more effectively than reading definitions. I imagine teaching in the style I have learned best.

 

How this impacts my approach to teaching

 

I was a student in Computer Science and I know how difficult the subject can be. However, I do believe that, a lot of the difficulty comes from the way the material is presented by the college professors. My advantage is that I can present the material more effectively, thus making the content more understandable to K12 students.

 

Valuing logic and pragmatism, I will adopt practices supported by reliable evidence into my teaching approach.

 

As an introvert, I will be courteous to my students. I will show them that I care about their well-being and learning without overstepping boundaries.

My Teaching Philosophy

I believe I am responsible for preparing students to become contributing members of society. This means helping them develop knowledge, skills, and maturity. I also believe there is a time for a flexible learning environment where my students are expected to give input on what they learn. My role would be a facilitator who helps them succeed. In addition to helping students grow academically, I also believe in helping them mature into respectful members of society.
